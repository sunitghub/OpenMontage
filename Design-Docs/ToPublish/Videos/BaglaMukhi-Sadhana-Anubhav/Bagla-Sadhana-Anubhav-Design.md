# Bagla Sadhana Anubhav Design

## Visual Bible

- Format: 16:9 YouTube.
- Style: polished Hindi mythological TV serial stills, clean devotional storybook digital painting, grounded interiors, readable characters.
- Base palette: dark devotional shadows, yellow room tones, saffron, marigold, lamp gold, soft smoke gray.
- Sacred focal rule: keep one dominant subject per frame: Maa Baglamukhi, the sadhak, the yantra, the diya, or the prayer offering.
- Local motion rule: motion should come from the focal point through glow, diya flicker, smoke drift, curtain movement, or slow camera push.
- Text rule: no generated text inside images; captions and titles belong in composition.

## Maa Baglamukhi Anchor

- Reference asset: `Maa-Baglamukhi-Anushthan.jpg`.
- Use it for iconographic direction: Maa Baglamukhi's yellow-gold identity, crown, jewelry, classic devotional poster style, golden gada, and blessing presence.
- Yanpeedha form: when showing power/victory iconography, Maa Baglamukhi may grip the tongue of a subdued asura adversary while holding the golden gada. The adversary should read as a mythological demon/asura, not a sadhu, guru, or ordinary devotee.
- Scene-1 anchor should be a wide divine-vision frame: large yellow-curtained room, Maa Baglamukhi hovering in the air, left palm stretched out in blessing, devotees represented only by raised hands or partial silhouettes, golden aura filling the room.
- Do not put a sadhu, guru, cave ashram, or full kneeling male devotee in the Scene-1 anchor image.
- First usable generated anchor should become the reference for later scenes where Maa's image appears on the bajot or as a subtle divine presence.
- Avoid horror styling, gore, distorted faces, extra limbs, or cluttered supernatural effects.

## Prompt Continuity

- MidJourney default: use `--ar 16:9 --style raw --s 80 --v 6` for this first visual pass.
- Competitor match: prefer palace-drama staging, warm practical lamps, red curtains, carved pillars, clean faces and hands, and grounded devotional scenes over cosmic deity-poster effects.
- Detail rule: add set dressing and ritual props where appropriate: marble or stone floor, carved pillars, red velvet curtains, brass lamps, copper plates, marigold flowers, textured yellow cloth, rice grains, incense holders, cloves, sandalwood bowl, brass bell.
- Keep the sadhak visually consistent: Indian male devotee, simple yellow dhoti, yellow shawl, seated posture, reverent expression.
- Keep the sadhana room consistent: empty home room, yellow walls, yellow curtain, low night lighting.
- Keep ritual objects consistent: wooden bajot, yellow cloth, yellow rice Baglamukhi Yantra, Maa Baglamukhi image, ghee diya, yellow flowers, seven incense mounds, cloves, yellow lota, kaner flowers.
- Prefer 1-2 strong stills per scene and reuse them with local motion rather than generating unrelated variations.

## Competitor Style Notes

- Follow the devotional competitor pattern: motion-light montage rather than all-video generation.
- Use slow zooms, pans, dissolves, smoke, glow, particles, and lamp flicker to create perceived richness.
- Reserve true video generation for later hero tests only after the stills look correct.
- For this script's current first pass, all scene artifacts are marked `Generate now: No`.
